Future of Drone Technology



Drones in Commercial Business

          With the continual expansion of technology many consumers are seeking for the most convenient business practices to indulge in. Companies have moved from paper to digital in the form of internet interaction. Similarly these companies are now moving from packages delivered through middlemen to using their own “men” or autonomously controlled robots.
          By removing the separate cost of traditional postal services, internet based companies can now appeal to more consumers by being able to sell without the need for two different charges on top of the subtotal; Shipping taxes.
          One company that is taking such an idea into the light uis Amazon. This company is innovating delivery services as we know it.Their experimental service “Amazon Prime Air” will allow customers to receive packages in less than 30 minutes after ordering.
          But just as drones can be beneficial to consumers, there are also safety and privacy concerns that can arise from bringing the technology to consumers, which has in turn delayed the introduction to such technology.

Other Drone Applications

          News outlets have begun to use inexpensive drones that are equipped with cameras to capture aerial footage. The entertainment industry have also begun using drones to shoot aerial perspective shoots, these relatively cheap drones have started to replace helicopters and cranes.
          Drones have also brought a new frontier of artistic expression, aerial photography. Blending photography and drone enthusiast bringing new and amazing aerial art of places.
          Wildlife biologists have started to use drones to monitor on different species in order to learn more about the animals without disturbing them as much as before. Unmanned drones called Gliders are used to collect valuable information about oceans and marine life.
